अथातो ब्रह्म जिज्ञासा। जन्माद्यस्य यतः। शास्त्रयोनित्वात्। तत्तु समन्वयात्। ईक्षतेर्नाशब्दम्। गौणश्चेन्नात्मशब्दात्। तद्धेतुव्यपदेशाच्च। स्वाप्ययात्। न चाधिकारिकमपि परनिराकरणं सर्वगतत्वाच्च।
योगश्चित्तवृत्तिनिरोधः। तदा द्रष्टुः स्वरूपेऽवस्थानम्। वृत्तिसारूप्यमितरत्र। अभ्यासवैराग्याभ्यां तन्निरोधः। तत्र स्थितौ यत्नोऽभ्यासः। स तु दीर्घकालनैरन्तर्यसत्कारासेवितो दृढभूमिः।
अथ योगानुशासनम्। योगः समाधिः। तच्छिद्रेषु प्रत्ययान्तराणि संस्कारेभ्यः। क्षीणवृत्तेरभिजातस्येव मणेर्ग्रहीतृग्रहणग्राह्येषु तत्स्थतदञ्जनता समापत्तिः। स्मृतिपरिशुद्धौ स्वरूपशून्येवार्थमात्रनिर्भासा निर्वितर्का।

The Great Gatsby

by F. Scott Fitzgerald

(4.5/5)

A classic novel by F. Scott Fitzgerald that explores themes of decadence, idealism, social upheaval, and excess, creating a portrait of the Jazz Age. Set in the summer of 1922, the novel tells the story of millionaire Jay Gatsby and his pursuit of Daisy Buchanan, a wealthy young woman whom he loved in his youth.

Published 1925
Pages 180
Genre Classic, Fiction
Language English

Sample from The Great Gatsby

In my younger and more vulnerable years my father gave me some advice that I've been turning over in my mind ever since.

"Whenever you feel like criticizing anyone," he told me, "just remember that all the people in this world haven't had the advantages that you've had."

He didn't say any more, but we've always been unusually communicative in a reserved way, and I understood that he meant a great deal more than that. In consequence, I'm inclined to reserve all judgments, a habit that has opened up many curious natures to me and also made me the victim of not a few veteran bores.
